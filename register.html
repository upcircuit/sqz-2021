DATA PRIVACY CLAUSE

The Office of Student Affairs (OSA) has required all UP organizations to comply with the Data Privacy Act of 2012, effective immediately. One of the many implications of this Act is that organizations are now legally required to obtain consent from their members before photographing them. While the Act states that “consent shall be evidenced by written, electronic or recorded means,” the OSA has stipulated that consent may only be given through written means.

By ticking 'I agree', you agree and give consent to SquEEEze and its sponsors to process your personal information (i.e. event documentation, attendance monitoring, session recording) on the event proper.

CATEGORY

Teams from top colleges and universities nationwide will compete once more in this year's EEE Virtual Quiz Show. In this competition, each team's abilities, strategy, and teamwork will be thoroughly tested and only the best will win.

This year's installment includes a revamped Technological Design Contest wherein students from across the country will be presenting their innovative ideas in the form of project proposals to address a certain national or social issue. The said issue to be tackled will be based on the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 7: Affordable and Renewable Energy.

In its sixth year, the SquEEEze High School Quiz Show will bring together high school students from all over the country to compete and test their intellect against each other. They will be introduced to the fundamentals of Electrical and Electronics Engineering through online lectures and webinars, and then test their Math and Science skills at the quiz show.

QUALIFICATIONS

1. A maximum of three (3) teams are allowed to join per school.

2. Each team shall be composed of three (3) students from the same school. The students must be currently enrolled and taking BS Electronics and Communications Engineering (ECE), BS Computer Engineering (CoE), or BS Electrical Engineering (EE) during the term that the students join the 24th SquEEEze.

3. A maximum of two (2) coaches are allowed per school.

a. The coaches must be bonafide faculty members of the same school the students of the team are enrolled in.

b. Coaches are allowed to register for more than one team in their school.

4. Letters of certification of the coaches' and participants' qualifications from the Dean of their respective schoos, colleges, or universities shall be submitted on or prior to the registration period on May 21, 2021, 11:59 PM.

ELIGIBILITY

• Each team may be made up of one, two, or three student Team Members. All participating Team Members must be currently enrolled in an undergraduate degree program in the field of electrical and electronics engineering (BS Electronics Engineering, BS Computer Engineering, BS Electrical Engineering).

• Eligibility may be verified at any time by the organizers; decisions concerning eligibility made by the Organizers are final. A student may only participate in one Team. Teams may edit their Member information by emailing the Organizers until two weeks before the competition.

• The official language of the competition is English. All competition requirements including registration, correspondence, and project proposal submission, must be accomplished in English. At least one student Team Member must speak fluently, and that member must be present during the event in order to conduct their team's presentation if they are selected as one of the Finalist Teams.

QUALIFICATIONS

1. A maximum of three (3) teams are allowed to join per school.

2. Each team shall be composed of three (3) students from the same school. The students must be in senior high school (Grade 11 or 12) during the term that the students join the 24th SquEEEze.

3. A maximum of two (2) coaches are allowed per school.

a. The coaches must be bonafide faculty members of the same school the students of the team are enrolled in.

b. Coaches are allowed to register for more than one team in their school.

4. Letters of certification of the coaches' and participants' qualifications from the Dean of their respective schoos, colleges, or universities shall be submitted on or prior to the registration period on May 14, 2021, 11:59 PM.

GUIDELINES

• The participants of the quiz bee are encouraged to join the workshop.

• The workshops are open to ALL Junior High School and Senior High School students.
